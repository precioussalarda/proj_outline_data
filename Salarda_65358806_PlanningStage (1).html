Methods and Plan:

K-Nearest Neighbours Classification can be used to predict subscribe (categorical) using the numerical variables age and played_hours because it directly outputs a class label. It is flexible with the data points, whether it has a linear pattern or not. KNN assumes similar data points of age and played_hours would be near, and it is also sensitive to the scaling of the distance. This method is sensitive to irrelevant predictors and the choice of K (over or underfitting). Especially with the zero values in this dataset, the predictions may be incorrect because of biased distance calculations.

The dataset will first be split into 75% training and 25% testing by randomly reordering and splitting into subsets with equal proportions of the labels. The model would be made and the best K determined from validation sets using cross-validation (5 folds) of the training data. To evaluate the classifier, predictions will be made using testing data. A confusion matrix will be generated, and evaluation metrics (accuracy, recall, and precision) will be calculated. In this case, a separate classifier can be made to predict players.csv without the zero values for comparison.
